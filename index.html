Traveling
Welcome everyone who loves traveling as I do.
I have visited 27 member states of the United Nations and 28 countries so far. Thanks to my work as a freelance and a constant passion for discovering new places I keep on expanding my list of visited counties. Right now it includes the following countries: Ukraine, Poland, Romania, Bulgaria, Greece, Serbia, Hungary, Slovenia, Italy, France, Monaco, Spain, Germany, Czech Republic, Egypt, Turkey, Macedonia, Austria, Vatican, Malta, Norway, Denmark, Sweden, Bosnia, Montenegro, Croatia, Belgium, Luxembourg, Netherlands.
The one upcoming is Belarus.
That is not even about reaching some point and crossing out new boarders in the map. But of course there is sort of fun in such things. I like traveling for the journey you make. It is a way that broads my mind, makes me rich with experience and memories. I meet unique people, see a different lifestyle and culture and that’s make me happy.
I don’t want to collect things. I try to do my best to fill my life with adventures. My moto is to tell people stories but no stuff to show.
Once my friend told me: “Die with memories – not dreams”.
My biggest hobby – traveling – helps me to stay positive and find inspiration in daily things. I see a beauty in the world and I want everyone to feel the same.

Teaching
I had been teaching programming for high school students for an amount of my lifetime.
As a beginning it was just a hobby. But suddenly my students showed a great enthusiasm and willing to absorb information – that motivated me a lot.
I hold the classes and I really enjoyed what I was doing. I guess my key to reach success was constant striving for progress. I didn’t desire to do it perfectly. I was learning from the mistakes.
By that time I had already gained some experience in the IT and I was happy to share my skills and knowledge. I found people who wished to develop in the field of computer science.
The more I taught for the students – the more new things I discovered myself and had a better understanding of programming.
